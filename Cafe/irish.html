Irish

Irish Coffee is one of the most beloved drinks in the history of Irish culture, with a warm and inviting flavor that’s sure to please even the pickiest of tastebuds.

Irish Coffee first occurs in written record on November 10th, 1952, when the New York Times reported on a new “special to Ireland” – Irish Coffee.

The article reads, “At Shannon Airport outside Limerick, passengers in the jet taxi of a parked Aer Lingus aircraft are served with a new Shannon Airport Special – Irish Coffee.”

So, it appears as though Irish Coffee as we know it today first appeared as a way to liven up the dullness of an airport.

Irish Coffee spread rapidly across the globe, becoming a mainstay of airline travel, restaurants, and taverns.

As the saying goes, “an Irish Coffee a day keeps the doctor away!”
